Jake Ciely’s FINAL 2019 fantasy football rankings, projections and auction values

By Jake Ciely Aug 31, 2019 1433

Welcome to your new favorite bookmarked webpage!

That’s right, the 2019 fantasy football season is fast approaching, and as always, the biggest (and – in my opinion, best) part of the Draft Kit are the rankings, projections and auction values. And this year it’s bigger than ever with nearly 400 rankings and projections. That includes all 32 DSTs and, of course, no kickers! So, 368 players projected and ranked (by Half-PPR). After an offseason spent obsessing, refining, researching, and generally putting these together, I’m just happy to see them in all of their glory.

[Check out The Athletic’s fantasy football draft kit for all your draft-day needs]

UPDATED DATE: Sept 4

Melvin Gordon has been and still is eight games. He could sit four, eight, a full 10 or even be on a new team. LeSean McCoy is similar to Carlos Hyde’s value before he was cut but with a decent boost. Damien Williams was RB3 over the final four games on just 15 touches per game!

Projections Settings:

Passing TD = 4pt
All Other TD = 6pt
25 Yards Passing = 1pt
10 Yards Rushing/Receiving = 1pt
Default Sort = Half-PPR
League/Budget = 12-team/$200

League Size Adjustment (rough estimate):
Moving up an additional two teams hits QB and RB mainly. Quarterbacks go up around 10%, running backs 5-10%, wide receivers barely at all 1-3% and tight ends barely more than receivers around 2-4%. Decreasing in teams by two drops: QB = 8-10%, RB = 10-12%, WR = 12-15%, TE = 2-5%. Tight ends barely move much across league sizes due to the significant drop-off from top-end picks to the rest and shallowness of the position. And again, this is an overall estimate of value changes, as it still waterfalls positionally – meaning, RB6 and RB20 both might drop $2, but the percentage difference for both would be much different.

Just a few quick notes on the logistics of everything. I’ll be updating this page instead of starting a new post every time I do an update (which will be fairly frequently), so keep coming back here to see the latest movement.

You can’t sort the value tables, but you can copy and paste the numbers into Excel or a Google Sheet, and even tweak projections (my feelings won’t be hurt) to sort on your own.

For aesthetics and general readability purposes, we’re at the column width limit, so these are the categories we’ll have all preseason. Fun tip we learned last season: if you’re having trouble scrolling right or left on the app, try opening this post with your phone’s web browser.

I will be all over the comments section, so any questions, please drop them below. This is what many of you are subscribed here for, and I don’t take lightly the responsibility of making sure we earn every cent you pay.

And now… away we go!
